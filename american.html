Caffè Americano or Americano

(shortened from Italian: caffè americano or American Spanish: café americano, literally American coffee)

is a style of coffee prepared by brewing espresso with added hot water, giving it a similar strength to, but different flavor from, drip coffee.

The strength of an Americano varies with the number of shots of espresso and the amount of water added.

The drink consists of a single
or double-shot of espresso brewed with added water.

Long Black is an Australasian term for an Americano
(in contrast to Short Black for espresso)
